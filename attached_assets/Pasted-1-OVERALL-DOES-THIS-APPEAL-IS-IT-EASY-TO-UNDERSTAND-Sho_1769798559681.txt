1️⃣ OVERALL: DOES THIS APPEAL & IS IT EASY TO UNDERSTAND?

Short answer:
✅ Yes — the value is now clear within ~5 seconds
⚠️ But you are slightly over-explaining in the middle
❌ The logo still signals “relationship/dating app,” not finance

Let’s unpack.

What’s working very well now

The hero headline is clear and grounded

“Shared expenses, made simple. Saving together, made possible.”
This is strong. This passes the “say it out loud to a friend” test.

The subheadline explains what the app actually does

The core loop is obvious: add → auto-handle → nudge → progress

“No bank linking required” is an excellent trust reducer

The tone is calm, not salesy — that’s perfect for money + couples

If a first-time visitor scrolls casually, they now understand:

“This helps us manage shared expenses easily and nudges us to save.”

That’s a win.

2️⃣ COPY & UX: WHERE TO TIGHTEN (IMPORTANT)

You don’t need more content.
You need slightly less repetition and sharper phrasing.

A. Hero section (minor tweak only)

Current:

Build Together automatically categorizes, splits, and tracks shared expenses — while an adaptive AI helps you and your partner stay on track toward your financial goals.

Issue:
“Adaptive AI” is still a bit abstract this early.

Suggested refinement (clearer, more human):

Build Together automatically categorizes, splits, and tracks shared expenses — and gently helps you and your partner stay on track toward your financial goals.

You can introduce “adaptive AI” later, which you already do well.

B. “How It Actually Works” section

This section is conceptually excellent.
But you’re doing both a 4-step and a 3-step explanation later.

That’s redundant.

Recommendation (very specific):

Keep ONLY ONE of these:

Either the 4-layer version (with Dream Guardian learning)

Or the simpler 3-step version

Do not keep both

For a landing page, I’d keep the 4-step version and delete the second “How It Works” block.

Why?
Because repetition makes users think:

“Wait… is this more complicated than I thought?”

Simplicity is as much about what you remove as what you say.

C. Dream Guardian explanation (this is strong, just trim)

This section is actually very good:

No lectures. No shame. Just steady guidance.

That line is excellent. Keep it.

Small improvement:

Reduce bullet count slightly

Focus on outcome, not mechanics

For example, instead of:

Observes Patterns

Learns What Works

Personalized Nudges

Adapts Over Time

You could compress to:

Learns what motivates you

Adjusts nudges over time

Keeps progress moving

Less technical. More emotional.

D. Testimonials

This line works very well:

“We finally stopped fighting about money.”

That’s gold.

Suggestion:
Eventually replace “Sarah & Mike” with:

“Early user”

or a short anonymized label

Fake-looking testimonials hurt trust. Early-stage honesty > polish.
