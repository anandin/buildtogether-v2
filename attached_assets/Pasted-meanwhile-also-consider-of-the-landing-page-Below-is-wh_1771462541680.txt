What Needs Work
Critical red flags on the CTA: The App Store and Google Play links appear to point to Expo Go, not an actual published app. If users click through and land in the generic Expo client, that's a trust-destroying experience. This alone could kill conversion. Either the app needs to be published natively, or this needs much clearer "coming soon" framing.
The "Dream Guardian" brand is doing too much. You mention it in 4+ different sections with slightly different framings — AI coach, companion, memory, nudge engine. It starts to feel like vaporware. Pick one primary metaphor and stick to it. Right now it reads like feature soup dressed up in emotional language.
No social proof whatsoever. No testimonials, no user count, no ratings, no couple photos or stories. For a product built entirely on trust (it handles your money and your relationship), this is a significant gap. Even a single credible quote would help enormously.
Target audience is too vague. "Couples" is a broad category. The copy oscillates between "partners," "you and your partner," and "families" (there's a Family Profile feature). Are you targeting romantic couples, married couples with kids, or roommates? This ambiguity bleeds into the feature descriptions and weakens the emotional resonance.
The screenshot section is static and hard to evaluate. The feature tabs (Dream Guardian, Expense Tracking, etc.) are listed but it's unclear how navigable they are — from the HTML, they appear to be separate image panels rather than a true interactive demo. An embedded video walkthrough or interactive prototype would dramatically increase engagement.
"Guardian Memory" transparency angle is undersold. This is actually a clever trust-building feature — showing users what the AI has learned about them. It deserves a callout higher in the page, not buried near the bottom. Especially in a post-ChatGPT world where people are anxious about AI, leading with "you can see exactly what we've learned" is a competitive differentiator.
Pricing is missing. "Free to start" is mentioned once in tiny text, but there's no pricing section. If there's a freemium model or a subscription, curious users will leave to look for it and likely not return.
Privacy section is not linked in the nav. You have a Privacy Promise as a product feature, but to actually read the policy users have to scroll to the footer. For a product that's explicitly not connecting to bank accounts and making privacy a key selling point, a visible "Privacy" nav link or dedicated section would reinforce trust throughout the scroll.
